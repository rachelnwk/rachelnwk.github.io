How To Play

Setup

To set up a game of Mahjong, start by shuffling 144 Mahjong tiles thoroughly facedown on the table. Each player then builds a wall by stacking the tiles facedown in rows, two tiles high and 19 tiles long, forming a square shape. The players then push the walls together to form a diamond pattern with a hollow center. Next, each player rolls the dice to determine their seating position and the starting point on the wall from which tiles will be drawn. The dealer, determined by the highest dice roll, breaks the wall and begins distributing tiles counterclockwise, each player receiving 13 tiles (14 if they are the dealer). The game then proceeds with players taking turns drawing and discarding tiles, aiming to complete specific sets such as three of a kind, sequences, or special hands, depending on the variation being played.

Types of Tiles


Bamboo
These tiles feature designs resembling bamboo sticks. Each tile typically displays a number of bamboo sticks corresponding to its value (from 1 to 9).

Dots
The Dots tiles are adorned with circles representing the numerical value from 1 to 9. The number of circles on each tile corresponds directly to its value.

Numbers
The Characters tiles depict Chinese characters or numerals from 1 to 9. Each tile is adorned with the appropriate character or numeral indicating its value.

Dragons
These are a special type of tile known as an Honor Tile. These tiles depict stylized representations of dragons. There are three types: Red, Green, and White.

Winds
These are a special type of tile known as an Honor Tile, and there are 4 types representing each direction: North, South, East, and West.
